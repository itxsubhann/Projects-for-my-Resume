Inaugural Address

Documentry

President George W. Bush's inaugural address on January 20, 2001, emphasized the themes of unity, freedom, and responsibility. He began his address by acknowledging the contentious election and the need to move beyond partisan divisions, stating that "we are not enemies, but friends."
Bush went on to discuss the importance of freedom and democracy, stating that "the survival of liberty in our land increasingly depends on the success of liberty in other lands." He also expressed his commitment to promoting education and compassion, saying that "we must be a compassionate, decent, and caring society."
Another key theme of Bush's inaugural address was responsibility, both personal and national. He challenged Americans to take responsibility for their own lives and communities, and also stressed the importance of national responsibility in areas such as foreign policy and economic prosperity.
Overall, Bush's inaugural address set the tone for his presidency, emphasizing unity and responsibility while also promoting a vision of America as a beacon of freedom and democracy in the world.
