An American president is the head of state and government of the United States of America. The president is elected by the American people every four years, and serves as the commander-in-chief of the military, chief diplomat, and the head of the executive branch of the government. The president's role includes setting national policy, managing the federal bureaucracy, and working with Congress to pass laws. The president also has the power to veto legislation and appoint judges and other officials. The presidency is one of the most powerful and influential positions in the world, with the ability to shape domestic and foreign policy and to lead the country through times of crisis.
